Title: "The Brave Guardian of Rightsville"
(Right to protection )

In a colorful town called Rightsville, there lived a young boy named Benny. Benny was known for his kindness and his deep sense of responsibility. He had a special job in the town-he was the Guardian of Rightsville. One sunny morning, Benny was playing with his friends in the park when he noticed something strange. A group of older kids were teasing a smaller boy, Timmy. They were taking away his toys and being unkind.


Benny knew that everyone in Rightsville had the right to be protected from harm and bullying. So, he decided to act. He walked over to the older kids and said firmly, "Stop! Timmy has the right to be safe and happy, just like all of us." The older kids were surprised by Benny's confidence. They stopped teasing Timmy and realized that Benny was right. Timmy thanked Benny with a big smile, and they became friends.


Benny's actions made the town of Rightsville even more special. Children knew that they had the right to be protected from harm, just like Timmy did. Benny became a hero in their eyes, the Guardian of Rightsville, who stood up for what was right. From that day forward, Benny and his friends made sure that everyone in Rightsville felt safe and protected. They knew that their rights were precious, and they would do whatever it took to defend them. And so, the story of Benny, the Brave Guardian of Rightsville, taught children that they have the right to be protected from harm and that it's important to stand up for those rights, just like Benny did for Timmy.
